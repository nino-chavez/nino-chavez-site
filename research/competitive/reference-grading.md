# Reference grading — competitive set

Stage 1 research register. Feeds the `research-reference-grader` gate.

**Track rules.** *Convention* = users recognize/expect this pattern (evidence: reach,
category dominance). *Quality* = meets formal design/content standards (evidence must be
named — design-press citations, awards, published design systems, expert recognition).
*Both* = qualifies on both. Quality claims in the Diagnose may ground **only** in
Quality/Both rows. Honesty rule applied: a popular reference with weak/utilitarian
craft-standards is graded Convention-only; a repo with no reach is graded Neither.

All references accessed 2026-07-08.

## One-line rationale per row

1. **simonwillison.net — Both.** Category-defining practitioner diary (Convention) authored
   by an independently press-recognized expert (Quality); cited for evidence-adjacency and
   the diary→guide move, with Quality scoped to content authority not visual design.
2. **promptingguide.ai — Convention.** Ubiquitous, category-dominant technique reference
   with no formal design/content-standard recognition; cited as the tool-first entry the
   pilot rejects and as a depth backstop only.
3. **Every.to genre — Both.** Recognized publication and recognized essay format (Convention)
   holding professional editorial standards and a widely-cited framing (Quality); cited for
   persona-naming and evidence-anchored workflow narrative.
4. **Anthropic Academy — Both.** Canonical, press-covered vendor academy (Convention) with
   professionally produced instructional design and certificates (Quality); cited for
   role-track split, self-serve enrollment, and the diagnosis-free catalog failure.
5. **ai-analyst-academy — Neither.** No reach (not Convention), no design/expert recognition
   and a stale CSR-only shell (not Quality); in scope only as a Q3 placement candidate and
   structural comparable, never as a citable authority.
