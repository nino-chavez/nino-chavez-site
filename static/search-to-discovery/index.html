01 The Problem → The Narrow View

The use case everyone reaches for — and why it sells the opportunity short

The Default Demo

User
"Find me laptops under $1,000"
Agent
"Here are 47 laptops under $1,000, sorted by rating..."

This is just search with a chat interface. A glorified filter. The agent adds no intelligence — it's a price finder with extra steps.

What This Misses

No context — What is the laptop for? Work? Gaming? Video editing?
No history — Has this customer bought from us before? What do they own?
No discovery — Maybe they need a desktop, or a tablet with keyboard
No advice — Which laptop solves their actual problem?

The real question: When a customer says "laptops under $1,000," are they asking for a product list — or are they starting a conversation about solving a problem they don't fully understand yet?

02 Elasticity in Discovery → The Customer Query

This is what a real discovery conversation looks like — not "robot vacuums under $500"

Customer

"Should I consider other brands? I have mostly hard floors with a few entry door rugs. I don't like how often I have to fill the small water tank in my current Roborock. I also prefer something with more budget-conscious cleaning solution that is easy to apply to the tank."

What a search engine hears:

robot vacuum
hard floors
water tank
cleaning solution

What an intelligent agent hears:

Frustration signal: Current product isn't meeting needs
Surface type: Hard floors primary, rugs secondary
Pain point: Tank refill frequency (maintenance burden)
Cost sensitivity: Ongoing consumables, not just upfront
Openness: Brand-agnostic, willing to switch
Implicit need: Less maintenance, more autonomy

9 / 12 Section 03
03 The Intelligence Layer → The Three-Step Framework

Discovery happens before search — search is just the final step

STEP 01

Identify Problem

Map fuzzy human language to structured "Jobs to be Done." Transform "I hate refilling the tank" into "User needs autonomous mopping with large-capacity dock."

This is the hardest part. This is where agentic intelligence lives.

STEP 02

Blend with Context

Apply domain constraints intelligently. What does Best Buy carry? What's in stock locally? What has this customer bought before? What do we know about their home?

Operations aren't filters — they're first-class inputs.

STEP 03

Deliver Answer

Only now does search begin — but it's not blind retrieval. It's highly-qualified matching. The solution space has already been narrowed from millions to dozens.

Search vendors solve step 3. The magic is in steps 1 and 2.

The Model Inversion: Traditional retail follows Search → Filter. Agentic commerce must be Filter → Discover. Operational data defines the boundary first. The agent intelligently guides customers within that boundary.

Your proprietary data is the competitive advantage — not the AI itself

What This Enables

"Based on your current Roborock, here's why you'll love the upgrade..."
"Customers who switched from Q7 Max to eufy X10 report..."
"Your Total Tech membership includes setup and installation..."

Generic AI is a tourist. Best Buy's agent is a local guide who knows your neighborhood.

The Core Thesis: If you don't own the intelligence layer, you become just another feed in a generic AI's database. Own what differentiates you. Buy what's commodity. Partner where you need access.
