# 01 — Diagnose (v2, hire-me pivot)

The original Stage 1 diagnose framed v3 as a balanced three-persona
practitioner studio surface (Peer Architect + Prospective Client +
Conference Circuit, equal weight). That framing produced a design that
showcases the practice but does not convert buyers.

Per direction 2026-05-25: **hire me is the primary goal**. This document
supersedes `01-diagnose.md` and re-derives the diagnoses through the
buyer-conversion lens. Other personas don't disappear — they become
secondary surfaces that support, not equal, the primary path.

## What changed in the framing

| Dimension | v0.1 (superseded) | v2 (this doc) |
|---|---|---|
| Primary goal | "Practitioner studio surface" | **Hire me** (convert buyers to engagements) |
| Primary persona | None — three balanced | **Prospective Client** |
| Site role | Showcase the practice | **Sell the practice** |
| Success metric | Receipt density visible | **Inquiries that convert to engagements** |
| Other personas | Peer Architect + Conference Circuit equal | Secondary surfaces serving authority/distribution |

## The single sentence (revised)

> The v2 ninochavez.co surfaces a polymath; the v3 surface needs to sell
> a hire-able architect. The practice is real, the receipts exist, and
> the buyer-cohort is identifiable — but no current surface tells a
> buyer what to buy, what it costs, or what they'd get for it.

## Primary persona — Prospective Client (promoted)

**Title cohort**: VP / Director / Head-of-X at buyer orgs
- Commerce platform owner at mid-to-large retailer
- Head of AI strategy at enterprise considering agent adoption
- Director of Engineering at a company evaluating methodology / agentic delivery
- Transformation PMO scoping vendor / advisor

**Decision pressure**: a meeting on their calendar where they need to
recommend a vendor, advisor, or speaker. Time-budget: 60 seconds on `/`,
3–5 minutes if not bouncing immediately.

**Decisive signal**: scheduled call (Cal.com / mailto with subject
template) OR LinkedIn message referencing a specific page.

**Engagement shapes the buyer is scoping for** (Nino's actual offerings —
verify in Stage 4):
1. **Methodology engagement** — apply Blueprint inside the buyer's org
2. **Advisory** — fractional architect / paid advisory hours
3. **Speaking** — keynote / panel / workshop
4. **Consulting** — bounded project work outside FT role

## Secondary personas (demoted)

| Persona | Role in v2 plan | Role in v2-hire-me |
|---|---|---|
| Peer Architect | Equal-primary, authority cohort | **Secondary** — provides Signal Dispatch traffic + cohort credibility that the Prospective Client cross-references. Surfaced via Signal Dispatch link + topics; does not get co-primary nav weight. |
| Conference Circuit | Equal-primary, distribution | **Secondary** — `/speaking` surface persists because speaking IS a hire-me revenue stream, but the page is a speaker-slate tool, not the primary funnel. |

## Diagnoses (re-derived for hire-me)

The original 8 diagnoses (D1–D8) addressed IA / copy / receipt density —
all still valid as background. **These 8 new diagnoses (B1–B8) name the
buyer-conversion failures specifically.** Background diagnoses from v0.1
remain in force where they don't contradict.

### B1 — No named services. Buyer cannot scan for what to buy.

**Evidence**:
- Current `/` prototype shows "Schedule a conversation" CTA without
  scoping what conversation
- Speaking page exists but doesn't differentiate from advisory or
  methodology engagements
- Aurvia (peer-cohort calibration, `competitive/peer-cohort.md`
  Archetype C) — Jen Anderson's fractional CTO site — has 4 named
  services with intake. v3 has zero.

**Why the gap exists**: framed as showcase, not sales. Showcase sites
don't list services; sales sites do.

**Prescription anchor** (Stage 2): Author a named-services section.
Candidates derived from Nino's actual engagement shapes:
- "Methodology engagement" (Blueprint applied to your org)
- "Architecture advisory" (fractional / hourly)
- "Keynotes + workshops" (named topics from /speaking)
- "Project consulting" (bounded outside-FT work)
Each service: one-sentence what / what-you-get / how-to-start.

### B2 — No named clients. Buyer can't verify delivery at scale.

**Evidence**:
- Of 11 receipts surfaced, 10 are Nino's own projects (Rally HQ, Signal
  X Studio, Quantifai, Photography, Pepper, Flickday, Blueprint,
  Atelier, Atelier Dashboard, Signal Dispatch)
- 1 is "BC Subscriptions — client work under NDA" — anonymous
- commerce.com day-job mentioned in subtext, not surfaced as client
- Zero buyer-recognizable enterprise logos or named case studies

**Why the gap exists**: Nino's client work is mostly under NDA. The
solution isn't to break NDAs — it's to surface what CAN be named
(commerce.com role explicitly, 630 Volleyball subcontracting, any
non-NDA engagements) + anonymized case shapes ("Fortune 500 commerce
platform — 47% cart-recovery lift via agent-driven personalization").

**Prescription anchor**: A "named clients" or "client work" strip with:
- commerce.com (FT role, explicit)
- 630 Volleyball (subcontractor, named)
- 2–3 anonymized case-shape cards ("Fortune 500 retail platform —
  [scope] — [outcome]")

### B3 — No photo of Nino. Buyer can't see who they'd be hiring.

**Evidence**:
- Current `/` has zero images of Nino
- `/speaking` has placeholder headshots but no source photo
- Buyer expectation: humans hire humans. A site with no face fails
  basic trust shape for advisory / consulting.

**Why the gap exists**: I skipped `gen-images` and treated photography
integration as a "later" task. For hire-me primary, headshot is a Stage 6
blocker.

**Prescription anchor**: Headshot in hero (right-side small avatar OR
left-side small portrait pulled from a Nino photo). Bigger photo on
`/about`. `/speaking` gets the headshot library it always needed.

### B4 — No availability framing. Buyer can't tell if Nino's hireable.

**Evidence**:
- Nino is FT at commerce.com per subtext
- No explicit statement about availability for outside engagements
- Buyer's obvious question: "are you actually available to hire?" gets
  no answer
- Cohort calibration: Aurvia has "Free discovery" + Calendly visible;
  Nino has "Schedule a conversation" with no availability signal

**Why the gap exists**: Avoided framing because the FT/side-practice
tension is ambiguous. But ambiguity costs more than clarity.

**Prescription anchor**: An "Availability" indicator on `/` near the
CTA. Three states (mirrors Cal.com availability endpoint patterns):
- **Available** — taking new advisory engagements this quarter
- **Limited** — selective engagements; brief inquiry first
- **Booked** — not taking new engagements through [date]; speaking only

Defaults to "Limited" — accurate for Nino's FT + side-practice shape.

### B5 — Generic single CTA. Buyer can't self-route to the right inquiry.

**Evidence**:
- Current `/` has one CTA: "Schedule a conversation"
- Buyer with a methodology engagement need clicks the same button as
  buyer with a speaking inquiry
- Inbound email lands with no subject pre-fill discriminating intent
- Triage burden falls on Nino, conversion friction on buyer

**Why the gap exists**: Single-path simplicity preferred at design.
But buyer-conversion benefits from explicit routing.

**Prescription anchor**: Replace single CTA with engagement-typed CTAs
near each services entry:
- "Inquire about methodology" → mailto with `subject=Methodology — [org]`
- "Inquire about advisory" → mailto with `subject=Advisory — [org]`
- "Book a speaking slot" → `/speaking` (slate-ready content)
- "Discuss a project" → mailto with `subject=Project — [org]`

Plus persistent header CTA that opens an engagement-type chooser modal
instead of a generic mailto.

### B6 — Receipts aren't ordered by buyer relevance.

**Evidence**:
- Current bento order: Rally HQ (volleyball) → Signal Dispatch
  (writing) → Blueprint (methodology) → Signal X Studio → Quantifai →
  Photography → supporting list
- A commerce buyer landing on this sees: "tournament platform" first.
  Volleyball, not commerce. They have to scroll past 4 cards to reach
  "commerce in the agent era" topics.
- Receipts are organized by Nino's recency-and-flagship, not by buyer
  vocabulary

**Why the gap exists**: Personal-portfolio ordering (what's exciting to
the maker) instead of buyer-portfolio ordering (what proves you can do
the thing they want to buy).

**Prescription anchor**: Re-order receipt list with commerce-architecture
proof first:
- commerce.com role (named) + any named commerce platform work
- BC Subscriptions (anonymized commerce case)
- Quantifai (AI FinOps — commerce-adjacent)
- Blueprint methodology
- Rally HQ as Blueprint's reference impl (NOT primarily as "tournament platform" — frame as "production app shipped via the methodology")
- Then everything else

### B7 — Speaking is mixed with showcase. Buyer can't tell it's a service.

**Evidence**:
- `/speaking` exists as a slate-ready page (good)
- But on `/`, speaking is just a nav link — not surfaced as a hireable
  engagement type
- Buyer scanning for "can I hire this person for a keynote?" needs to
  click into /speaking to find out it's an offer, not just bio + topics

**Why the gap exists**: Speaking treated as showcase ("here's the
substrate for talks") rather than service ("here's what you can book").

**Prescription anchor**: On `/`, treat speaking as one of the 4 named
services in the new services section. /speaking page deepens it.

### B8 — Pricing / engagement scope is invisible.

**Evidence**:
- No pricing on the site
- No scope shape (hourly vs project vs retainer vs flat-fee speaking)
- Buyer with budget constraint cannot self-qualify
- Cohort: Aurvia doesn't show explicit pricing but shows engagement
  shape ("priced for the stage you're at" + "Free discovery"). v3 has
  neither.

**Why the gap exists**: Pricing is fluid for outside-FT engagements.
But scope shape is stable and discoverable should be possible without
revealing rates.

**Prescription anchor**: Each named service gets a scope-shape line:
- Methodology engagement: "8–12 week scoped engagement, fixed scope, paid milestones"
- Advisory: "Fractional / hourly retainer, $X/hour starting"
- Speaking: "Keynote / 60-min workshop / panel — see /speaking for topics"
- Project consulting: "Bounded scope, 4–8 weeks typical, project quote"

(Nino verifies actual scope language in Stage 4. The point is scope
LANGUAGE not exact numbers.)
