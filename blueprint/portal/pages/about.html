Who · the lede

I work at the intersection of product architecture, agentic-software engineering, and a slowly-codified discipline I now call context engineering. The day job is at commerce.com, where I architect against BigCommerce-platform systems. The orbit around it is what this site exists to surface.

The orbit: a public methodology (github.com/nino-chavez/blueprint), a six-lathe production line (github.com/nino-chavez/forge), a 275-artifact writing corpus at blog.ninochavez.co, and a portfolio of agent-assisted side projects ranging from a 12-tool MCP architecture (Atelier) to a tournament-management platform paired with its own blueprint deploy (rally-hq.app).

What changed · the inflection

The v2 of this site framed the work as polymath — software, photography, music, and writing as four co-equal interests in a 2×2 grid. That framing was honest about the breadth but wrong about the structure. The breadth isn't four parallel interests; it's a single discipline (build agent-assisted systems that produce verifiable artifacts) with documentation surfaces in different media.

The shift is encoded in ADR-0001 on this repo: context-engineer positioning over polymath framing. The receipts followed — the forge umbrella went public, the blueprint methodology got formal staging (diagnose → prescribe → design → portal), and the surface area of the work went from 9 case studies on v2 to 8 clusters spanning 69 repos + 17 deploys + 275 artifacts on v3. The cluster IA on /work exists because the v2 surface hid 88% of the work.

What now · the practice

The practice has four operational layers: declarative rules in ~/.claude/CLAUDE.md, prompt-time hooks that load context (the Poe voice stack arrives this way), stop-time audits that catch hesitation patterns before they ship, and session-mining tools that rebuild the Poe stack from every prior transcript. The hesitation-fold diagram on /practice is what it looks like wired together.

The instrumented part is the point. The agents aren't doing the thinking — they're doing the executing, against a stack that predicts where I'd want to redirect and catches places I haven't redirected explicitly. Every claim on this site grounds to a real repo path or commit; that's the only positioning that survives contact with someone evaluating whether the receipts are real.
