Nino Chavez is a Product Architect at commerce.com working at the intersection of enterprise commerce architecture and AI-native software delivery. His thesis — architect-directs-agents — has been applied at production scale across multiple shipped products and forms the substrate for Blueprint, his brownfield audit-driven methodology for shipping AI-augmented systems through diagnose → prescription → design brief → fact-check stages.

His shipped work includes Rally HQ, a live tournament platform serving real volleyball tournaments built as the Blueprint Pattern B reference implementation; Atelier, an OSS template and interop protocol for mixed human/agent teams; Quantifai, an AI FinOps platform for cost attribution across model providers; and Signal X Studio, the identity surface for the practice. He also subcontracts B2B subscription work and runs adjacent brand surfaces (Let's Pepper, Flickday Media) and a 20,000-photo volleyball action gallery.

Nino writes at Signal Dispatch (277 essays since 2018) on harness engineering for commerce, the buy-vs-build threshold in the agent era, brownfield-over-greenfield methodology, and the practice of directing agents at production scale. He's based in Chicago and available for keynotes, panels, podcasts, and internal summits.
